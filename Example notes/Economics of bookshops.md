Visited a small #waterstones #book shop in Dorking today May 13th, got recommended to buy [[Train Dreams]] by Denis Johnson, which is a short novel, and alsp bought [[Travels with Epicurus]] which is about fulfilment and old age

Asked questions about the economics
The shop manager said that the average small bookshop should turn over £500,000 of books per year. If a bookshop is open 300 days a year, that means selling £1,666 of books a day!
Probably 100 books a day! I bought two. 12 books an hour ish. A book every 5 minutes.

He said the busiest times for selling are Autumn ("back to school" - also I imagine the weather starts to get colder) and Then Christmas. The worst time for selling is April May June - so maybe this is how we rationalise the idea of selling a book every 5 minutes with the limited activity in the shop!

He said 60-70% of the books that people left the shop with were bought there and then, the other portion are those that were online purchases which were collected from the store.

Also interesting is that bookshops are organised into hubs - the small Dorking store is part of a broader hub centralised around a big Guildford store

He said that older people (over 60) loved crime novels. Good to have this view reinforced. They also have to change books to keep up with trends a lot, which is a shame but obvious
