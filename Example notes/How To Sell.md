Qualification is critical:
- some good signs are having high % of computer vision staff, VC funding
- what are the other ways you qualify customers?

Structure:
- introductions
- prospect's current process
- pain points & ideal solution/requirements
- Our solution
- Next steps & timeline

Before the call:
- get a sense of the org structure, how senior the prospect is, and whose approval they would need

Introductions:
- Ask if now is still a good time to chat
- Introduce yourself
- Run through the above structure... 'and run through next steps if that makes sense'
	- 'Is there anything else that would be useful for you to run through?'

Current process:
- 'How do you currently do X'
	- learn the exact process
- Be prepared to recommend a competitor (and learn what they do well, relatively)
- Form hypotheses for what's problematic in their current process

Pain points:
- Understand what's broken about their current processes & the 1-3 things they care about most
- What's the biggest thing you want to fix about X?
- How come we're on this call?
- If you had a magic wand, what would the perfect solution look like? 
	- Or the top three things that come to mind.
- What's important to you in the ideal version of this process?
- Only suggest ideas if they are struggling.

Solution: 
- Explain why your a good fit based on what they said
- "Do you have any questions about us, have you had a chance to read up, or do you want a quick 1 minute intro and we can go from there"
- Confirm the 1-3 things they care about back to them, and then explain how we meet those needs, with credible proof points

Next steps:
- "What does the next steps process look like for you? I'm curious to know what other information I can give you, so your team can be confident that this is the best option"
- Find who they need to talk to and what they need from them
- "Would a follow-up chat next X work?"
- Ideally you want to know who needs convincing, how to convince that person, how to get on their calendar

Follow-up:
- Next day
	- Short, gracious, CTA
		- "You mentioned a call next Monday afternoon would be good; I’ll send an invite for 3.30pm and just let me know if you’d prefer a different time"
- Follow up relentlessly: every 3 days — you want to get a clear “no” or a clear “yes” or a clear “I’m doing XYZ and it will take me 5 days, follow up next week”
