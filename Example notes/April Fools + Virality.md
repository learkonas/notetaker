-   According to [Ernest Dichter](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ernest_Dichter), there are 4 motivations for someone to talk about a product.
    -   Product-involvement → First-hand experience using the product and the experience is so good and pleasurable that it must be shared. **Novel Product Experience: unique or innovative**
    -   Self-involvement → Sharing a product to gain attention, social capital or status. **Signalling, first movers**
    -   Other-involvement→ Expressing neighbourliness, caring, and friendship to others. **Who do people care about: friends + family; those suffering from injustice (or misfortune)**
    -   Message-involvement → Entertainment value or originality of ads/messaging is so humorous/informative that it deserves sharing. **Pop culture**

Psychologists have spent a lot of time trying to [answer](https://pubsonline.informs.org/doi/abs/10.1287/mnsc.2016.2476?casa_token=4S8c0tFXydMAAAAA%3A6QIizbZJ0dNUKZxpL-uaAjnhDBIsfpAeGEbyTlA-Y0IKUWvR3mGy7NdOnXEA9e0AXvyPvMKiQ9kpyg&) these questions. After blowing through millions of dollars of endowments money on behavioural studies, researchers in this domain (more or less) agreed on the factors most likely to influence the shareability of a product and it boils down to these four:

-   **Social Capital:** Confidence of being socially rewarded
    
-   **Social Risk:** Risk of being socially ignored or worse how they will be perceived or judged for recommending your product
    
-   **Referral incentive:** How strongly does the person desire the reward your are offering
    
-   **Effort Required:** How easy/hard is it to refer and get the reward?
